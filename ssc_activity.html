Impacts on Earth by celebrating traditional holi

  • Holi festival have harmfull effects on environment like noise pollution and water pollition etc.

  • All the dyes we use are chemically prepared and cause negative imapacts on environment.

  • An alleged environmental issue related to the celebration of traditional Holika bonfire, which is beleived to contribute to deforestation. Holika causes 30,000 bonfires every year, with each one burning approximately 100 kilograms of wood.

  • The use of heavy metal-based pigments during Holi is also reported to cause temporary wastewater pollution, with the water systems recovering to pre-festival levels within 5 days.

Impacts on Earth by celebrating eco-friendly Holi

  • The fake colours used during holi are chemical based and colours are not only very harmful to your skin, eyes, and hair but can also cause permanant disorder to our organs. So, we should prefer homemade and natural colours over chemical colours during holi

  • The remains of water ballons cause nothing but environmental pollution. We should say 'NO' to water ballons and plastic bags and motivate others.

  • Holi is a festival of fun. don't ruin it by using harmful and irrelevant products like petrol, oil paints, mud and other chemical based products. These products are hazardous to not only to your health but also to the environment.
